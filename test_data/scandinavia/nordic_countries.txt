# Scandinavian Countries

The Scandinavian countries include Denmark, Norway, and Sweden. Sometimes Finland and Iceland are also considered part of Scandinavia, though this broader region is more accurately called the Nordic countries.

## Geography
The Scandinavian Peninsula is the largest peninsula in Europe, extending from the Arctic Circle to the North and Baltic Seas. The region features dramatic landscapes including fjords, mountains, forests, and thousands of islands.

## Culture
Scandinavian countries are known for their high standard of living, social welfare systems, and commitment to equality. They share cultural similarities in design aesthetics, cuisine, and folklore.

## Languages
Scandinavian languages belong to the North Germanic language family, with Danish, Norwegian, and Swedish being mutually intelligible to varying degrees. Finnish belongs to the Finno-Ugric family and is unrelated to the Scandinavian languages.
